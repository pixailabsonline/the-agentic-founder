# Writing Rules — Human Voice
## Applied to all coach emails, follow-ups, lead magnet, landing copy

---

## 1. BANNED WORDS — delete on sight

**AI filler verbs**
navigate, foster, leverage, empower, elevate, unleash, harness, streamline, showcase, ensure, resonate, facilitate, utilize, optimize, cultivate, spearhead, champion, drive, enable, deliver, execute, implement, transform

**AI adjectives**
impactful, meaningful, transformative, holistic, dynamic, innovative, robust, comprehensive, seamless, cutting-edge, game-changing, actionable, scalable, sustainable, authentic (as a buzzword), genuine (as a buzzword), powerful (as a buzzword), crucial, vital, critical (when used for emphasis not fact)

**AI nouns / metaphors**
journey (as metaphor), space (as industry), ecosystem, landscape, paradigm, framework (when used generically), solution, offering, toolkit, playbook, blueprint

**AI filler phrases — cut entirely**
- "In today's world..."
- "Now more than ever..."
- "In order to..." → just "to"
- "It is important to note that..."
- "It is worth noting..."
- "As we navigate..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Let's explore..."
- "I'd like to take a moment to..."
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Don't hesitate to reach out"
- "Feel free to..."
- "As per my last email..."
- "Going forward..."
- "Circling back..."
- "Touch base"
- "Take the next step"
- "On this journey"
- "Move the needle"
- "Bring to the table"
- "Research shows..." (without citation)
- "Studies suggest..." (without citation)
- "Experts agree..." (without citation)
- "As a [X], you know..." opener

## 2. BANNED CONSTRUCTIONS

**The "not just X, it's Y" formula**
"Gravitas is not just a tool, it's a transformation."
Cut it every time. Say what it is. Don't contrast it with what it isn't.

**Symmetrical paragraph lengths**
AI writes paragraphs of nearly identical length. Break this deliberately.
Short paragraphs land hard. One sentence. Sometimes two words.
Then a longer one that earns the space.

**Tripling**
Never three parallel clauses in a row: "X, Y, and Z" pattern reads as AI immediately.
Two is fine. Three is a tell.

**The "As a [X]" opener**
"As a coach, you know..." — AI pretending to understand the reader's world.
Either you know their world specifically (from research) or you don't. No shortcut.

**Conclusory paragraph summaries**
Restating at the end of a section what you just said in it.
If you said it, don't say it again. The reader just read it.

**Transition word bridges**
Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, Importantly, Ultimately, In conclusion, To summarise, In essence, Notably, Interestingly — all AI connective tissue.
Human writing stops one thought and starts the next. No bridge.

**Passive voice**
"It has been shown", "this can be seen", "leaders are often told."
Something happened. Say who did it. Active always.

**Nominalizations — verbs turned into nouns**
"The achievement of goals" → "achieving goals"
"The development of leaders" → "developing leaders"
"The improvement of performance" → "improving performance"
Nominalizations slow everything down and sound bureaucratic.

**Hedging qualifiers**
somewhat, rather, quite, fairly, slightly, perhaps (when avoiding commitment)
Either say the thing or don't.

**Double adverbs**
truly remarkable, deeply meaningful, highly effective, incredibly powerful
One modifier. Never two stacked.

**Fake urgency**
"Don't miss out", "limited time", "act now", "before it's too late"
Not in professional copy. Ever.

**Exclamation marks**
One per document maximum. Usually zero.

**Em-dashes (—)**
Never. In any copy. Use a full stop, a new sentence, or nothing. Em-dashes are the single most common AI tell in professional writing.

**"I" or "We" as the opening word**
Of any email, any paragraph, any section.
Start with them, with the problem, with a fact.

## 3. STRUCTURAL TELLS

**Every paragraph the same length**
Vary it deliberately. A one-sentence paragraph after a five-sentence paragraph creates rhythm and emphasis that AI cannot fake.

**Every paragraph ends with a complete thought**
Human writers cut mid-idea sometimes. The paragraph break is the emphasis.

**Bullet lists in cold emails**
AI's default lazy structure. Never in a cold email. Bullets signal template.

**Bold text in plain-text emails**
Same problem. Signals template, not person.

**Three-part structures everywhere**
"First... Second... Third..." — AI loves a trinity. Break it.

**The setup-payoff-summary loop**
"Tell them what you'll say, say it, tell them what you said."
Cut the setup and the summary. Just say it.

---

## 4. WHAT HUMAN COPY ACTUALLY DOES

**Opens mid-thought**
As if picking up a conversation already in progress. No preamble.

**Names a specific thing**
A real framework name. A real phrase from their site. A real number. A specific moment.
Specificity is the fastest trust signal there is.

**Has one point**
Not three. One sharp observation. One implication. One ask.
Everything else is noise.

**Uses the reader's language**
Their words, their framing, their thesis. Not yours imposed on them.

**Earns the next sentence**
Every sentence exists to make the reader want to read the next one.
Cut anything that doesn't do that job.

**Lets silence carry weight**
A short paragraph after a long one.
A question with no answer following it.
White space is not failure. It is technique.

**Ends cleanly**
No trailing summary. No "I look forward to hearing from you."
No "best regards" followed by title and three social media links.
Say the last true thing and stop.

---

## 5. THE DETECTION TEST

Before sending, read the copy aloud. Flag any sentence where you:
- Slow down because the structure feels stiff
- Notice you said the same thing twice
- Hear a word you would never say in conversation
- Feel a transition that exists only to connect, not to add

If three or more sentences in a row have exactly the same rhythm, rewrite them.

If you can delete a sentence and the paragraph reads better, delete it.

---

## 6. THE CLARE ALLEN TEST

Before sending any email, ask one question:

Does this read like someone who spent 20 minutes on their website and found the one thing that is genuinely specific to them?

If yes: send.
If no: find the specific thing first. The email cannot be written without it.

## 7. APPLIED FORMAT — coach cold emails

```
[First name],

[The specific gap — one or two sentences, their language, threat first.
The problem they feel but haven't named. No framework yet.]

[Their framework] got them closest. [What it still can't do — one sentence.]

[Gravitas closes that gap — specific, timestamped, what to fix before the moment.]

I'm offering ten free analyses for the leaders you coach.

pixailabs.com/gravitas

Peter Idah
```

RULES:
- Name and comma only. No "Hi". No "Dear". CEOs write this way. Executive coaches will recognise it.
- Threat lands before the framework name — always
- No em-dashes. Ever. Use nothing or a full stop.
- No LinkedIn in sign-off
- Sign-off is "Peter Idah" only. No title, no company, no social links.

## 8. THE MISSING SIGNALS — what separates not-flagged from actually-human

**Burstiness**
Humans write in bursts. Short sentence. Then a longer one that builds on it and earns its length before cutting back to something blunt. AI smooths everything to medium. Rule: after every long sentence, ask whether the next one should be short. If three sentences in a row are the same length, break one.

**Consistent complexity (entropy)**
AI writes every sentence at roughly the same difficulty level. Human writing spikes — a technical observation followed by a blunt one-liner, followed by something conversational. Read the piece and mark the complexity of each sentence. If they are all the same, rewrite.

**The missing opinion**
AI hedges every claim to avoid being wrong. Humans take positions. Every piece must contain at least one statement someone could disagree with. If everything in the copy is safe and agreeable, it reads as AI. Say the uncomfortable thing. Name the industry problem directly. Make a claim.

**Idiomatic imprecision**
Humans occasionally use a slightly loose word — not a mistake, but the natural approximation of speech: "roughly", "something like", "somewhere around." AI is always precise. One deliberate approximation per piece reads human. Exact numbers everywhere reads like a press release.

**Incomplete thoughts that trust the reader**
AI always finishes its sentences. Humans sometimes let the implication hang and trust the reader to follow. The gap between what is said and what is obvious is what creates the feeling of being understood — not explained at.

**Contractions**
In emails: always use contractions. "It's" not "It is." "Don't" not "Do not." "You're" not "You are." Avoiding contractions in personal copy signals template immediately.

**Write as a person, not a company**
"I built this" not "we built this." "I read every reply" not "our team is here to help." In cold emails from Peter Idah, there is no "we." There is only one person who built this, and he is writing to one person who might use it.
