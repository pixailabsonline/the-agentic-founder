# Existing Solutions: Exit Planning and M&A Advisory for Professional Services Firms

Research conducted July 2026. Focus: what exists for 8-figure founder-owners of expertise businesses (consulting, advisory, specialist services) approaching exit or succession.

---

## CATEGORY 1: EXIT PLANNING / SUCCESSION ADVISORY

---

### Built to Sell / Value Builder (John Warrillow)

**What they promise:**
"We help business owners build companies that are sellable, even if you have no intention of selling." The pitch is around systematising and de-personalising the business to increase transferable value.

**What they actually deliver:**

The product set is largely self-serve assessments and content:

- *Value Builder Report* — an estimate of company value plus a roadmap for improvement. Online assessment tool.
- *PREScore (Personal Readiness to Exit Score)* — a psychological readiness assessment. Identifies whether the owner is emotionally prepared to exit.
- *Freedom Score* — financial readiness assessment. Can you sustain your lifestyle post-exit?
- *VidGuide* — a process documentation tool. "The fastest way to make your business run without you."
- *eBooks* — three titles on value drivers, SOPs, exit planning. Free.
- *Built to Sell Radio* — weekly podcast with exit interviews. Free.

The monetisation model appears to be primarily through the **Value Builder Advisor Program**: advisors (financial planners, accountants, business coaches) license the Value Builder methodology and assessments to use with their own clients. The platform is B2B2C — Warrillow trains and sells to advisors, who then sell to business owners.

**Who it's for:**
The content and tools are generic to any business owner considering exit. No specific focus on professional services or expertise businesses. The advisor program targets coaches and wealth managers who want a systematic framework to add to their practice.

**What it costs:**
Pricing is not publicly disclosed on either site. Advisor licensing is by application/intro call.

**What it does NOT address:**
- The founder-as-product problem. The entire methodology is built on making businesses owner-independent — but for expertise businesses where the founder IS the product (the judgment, the reputation, the relationships), the premise is either impossible or requires a fundamentally different approach.
- No acknowledgment that some expertise businesses are deliberately built around founder reputation as a competitive moat, and that exit requires transferring credibility, not just systematising processes.
- No IP-extraction or knowledge-encoding framework.
- No guidance on how a buyer values intangible judgment or proprietary methodology.
- UK/European market gaps and cultural differences.

### Collective 54 (Greg Alexander)

**What they promise:**
"Build the AI-native firm. Grow it deliberately. Exit on your terms." A founder-only community for boutique professional services firms working through growth, scale, and exit.

**What they actually deliver:**
A mastermind membership community with:
- Weekly peer group working sessions (live, founder-only, small group)
- Quarterly in-person workshops focused on Grow, Scale, or Exit tracks
- Annual Reunion (full community, confidential, no spectators)
- Curated content and insights
- Peer intelligence — the premise is that problems compound when experienced founders share lessons

This is explicitly a *community intelligence* model, not a consulting or advisory relationship. No deliverables in the traditional sense.

**Who it's for:**
- Founders and CEOs of boutique professional services firms
- Revenue $5M to $100M
- North America (US/Canada)
- Deliberately working toward exit within a 1-5 year horizon

This is the closest existing fit for the ICP in question, in terms of revenue range and firm type.

**What it costs:**
$1,700/month + $3,500 initiation fee (confirmed on join page). Approximately $24K/year all-in.
Membership is by introduction and intro call.

**What it does NOT address:**
- The founder-as-product problem specific to expertise businesses. Collective 54 includes all professional services (IT services, marketing agencies, staffing firms) — not specifically founder-owned advisory practices built on personal judgment.
- No individual coaching or advisory relationship. The value is peer learning, not expert guidance on your specific situation.
- No structured IP extraction or methodology transfer process.
- No deliverables that help a founder articulate or transfer their proprietary judgment to a buyer.
- North America only. No UK/European market focus.
- Does not address the founder's existential and identity questions about what comes next.

### Succession Plus (now at succession.plus)

**What they promise:**
"Maximise value, retain key people, and transition on your terms." Exit and succession planning for Australian business owners.

**What they actually deliver:**
Four service lines:
- *Business Valuation* — 60+ page Business Insights Report analysing 300+ data points. Financial analysis, operational assessment, industry benchmarking, value gap analysis. Delivered via a 2-week process with a strategy workshop. Uses their proprietary Capitaliz platform for ongoing tracking.
- *Succession and Exit Planning* — design an exit strategy. No specific process detail publicly available.
- *ESOP (Employee Share Ownership Plans)* — implementation for businesses wanting to transfer ownership to employees. 3-6 weeks typical.
- *M&A Advisory* — buy/sell transactions.

**Who it's for:**
"Profitable Australian SMEs with stable cash flow." General SME focus — not specific to professional services or expertise businesses.

**What it costs:**
Not publicly disclosed. Book a consultation.

**What it does NOT address:**
- Geographic limitation: Australia. Not UK/Europe focused.
- Generic SME frame — no specific methodology for founder-dependent expertise businesses.
- No coaching or capability-building for the founder going through transition.
- The business valuation product (300 data points) doesn't account for how a founder's personal credibility or methodology gets valued and transferred.

### Axial

**What they promise:**
A private deal platform connecting sellers with qualified buyers. "Powerful deal marketing built around privacy, customisation, control, and seller confidentiality."

**What they actually deliver:**
Axial is a marketplace/platform, not an advisor:
- Matching algorithm connecting sellers to 4,500+ financial investors and acquirers (PE, family offices, independents)
- NDA execution and document sharing tools
- Data on buyer deal criteria, reputation, past transactions
- Advisor matching: connects sellers with vetted M&A advisors from their network of 2,000+ professionals
- Business valuation calculator

The actual selling process is handled by an M&A advisor (from Axial's network) who then uses the platform for buyer reach.

**Who it's for:**
North American lower middle market companies, revenue $2.5M-$250M, EBITDA $250K-$25M. Sectors include business services but no specific professional services or expertise firm focus.

**What it costs:**
Platform pricing not disclosed. Advisors on the platform charge typical M&A advisory fees (retainer + success fee, commonly 1-5% of transaction value for deals under $50M).

**What it does NOT address:**
- The platform assumes there is a sellable asset to market. For expertise businesses, this is precisely what's in question.
- No pre-sale preparation, IP structuring, or value creation work.
- No consideration of founder-dependent businesses or how to address key-person risk in a buyer process.
- No guidance on structuring the founder's ongoing role post-acquisition (earnout, consulting agreements) which is often the critical issue for expertise firm sales.

### Exit Planning Institute (CEPA certification)

**What they promise:**
"Align the owner's business, personal, and financial goals for maximum impact." The EPI trains advisors (financial planners, CPAs, attorneys) to deliver exit planning to business owners via the Value Acceleration Methodology.

**What they actually deliver:**
The EPI is primarily a **certification body for advisors**, not a service provider to business owners directly. It offers:
- CEPA (Certified Exit Planning Advisor) credential — training and exam for advisors
- Value Acceleration Methodology — a framework advisors use with clients
- Find-a-CEPA directory — business owners can find a CEPA near them
- State of Owner Readiness Report — research/content

The Value Acceleration Methodology works through:
- Identifying risks to business value (structural, operational, personal)
- Building social, customer, human, and structural capital
- Aligning financial goals with business and personal goals

Over 8,000 advisors hold the CEPA credential. The institute's customer is the advisor, not the business owner.

**Who it's for:**
Advisors who want a systematic framework for exit planning. Business owners interact with the EPI indirectly through their CEPA-credentialed advisor.

**What it costs:**
CEPA training pricing not on homepage. Business owners pay their individual CEPA advisor at whatever rate that advisor charges.

**What it does NOT address:**
- No direct service to business owners. The quality and depth of service depends entirely on the individual CEPA advisor the owner hires.
- No vertical specialisation. The Value Acceleration Methodology is generic. It doesn't account for expertise businesses where the "structural capital" question is the hardest problem.
- The 8,000+ CEPAs are mostly generalist advisors (financial planners, wealth managers) whose primary income is from financial products — exit planning is often a client acquisition strategy, not a deep operational engagement.
- No framework for IP extraction, knowledge transfer, or personal brand transition.

### FP Transitions

**What they promise:**
"End-to-end solutions for transforming businesses of sustainable value." Succession, M&A, valuation, and consulting for independent advisory firms.

**What they actually deliver:**
Four service lines for RIAs and wealth management practices:
- *Valuation* — "bespoke" valuations accounting for recurring revenue, profit margins, growth rates, retention. 15,000+ valuations over 20 years. Benchmarking against "perfectly matched cohort." Explicitly not one-size-fits-all.
- *Succession Planning* — designing multi-generational team structures, compensation strategy, equity arrangements
- *M&A* — 100+ transactions annually, marketplace for RIA buyers and sellers, legal documentation (the firm drafted 54 custom legal documents for one client)
- *Consulting* — transforming advisory businesses for long-term sustainability

**Who it's for:**
Exclusively RIAs and wealth management firms. Highly vertical — they have built deep domain expertise in one specific type of expertise business (financial advisory). 25+ years, 17,000+ clients.

**What it costs:**
Not publicly disclosed. Range from "turnkey, low-cost" to "concierge support."

**What it does NOT address:**
- Exclusively for regulated financial advisory firms. Not applicable to consulting firms, leadership advisors, or other expertise businesses.
- The RIA model has structural advantages that don't translate: recurring revenue via AUM, regulated licensing, established buyer universe. Most expertise businesses don't have these.
- Does not address the founder identity or psychological transition question.

**Key insight:** FP Transitions is what good looks like for a vertical — deep industry expertise, real M&A transactions, multi-service, 25 years of data. Nothing equivalent exists for consulting firms, leadership advisory practices, or knowledge-intensive boutiques.

## CATEGORY 2: M&A ADVISORY FOR PROFESSIONAL SERVICES / BOUTIQUE FIRMS

---

### Oaklyn Consulting

**What they promise:**
"A different kind of investment banking advisor." Boutique M&A advisory for deals too small or complex for traditional investment banks.

**What they actually deliver:**
- M&A advisory for mergers, acquisitions, capital raises, recapitalisations, joint ventures
- Flexible hourly fee models (not just success-fee-only)
- Financial modelling and valuation analysis
- Buyer/seller representation and negotiation
- Limited-scope engagements tailored to client budgets
- Track record: 150+ clients, 50:50 close-to-pass ratio

**Who it's for:**
- Founder and family-owned businesses
- VC portfolio companies
- Nonprofits considering strategic transactions
- Organisations with deals "too small for traditional investment banking"

**What it costs:**
Not publicly disclosed. Hourly billing mentioned — suggests more accessible pricing than traditional investment banking.

**What it does NOT address:**
- Generalist M&A advisor — no specific methodology for expertise businesses where the founder IS the asset.
- No pre-sale preparation or value creation work. They engage when you're ready to transact.
- No IP extraction, knowledge codification, or personal brand transfer framework.
- No guidance on founder role post-acquisition.

### Generational Group / Generational Equity

**What they promise:**
"Selling a business is more than a transaction — it's a defining moment." Claims over $9 billion in transactions. "Plan Smart, Sell Smart" process.

**What they actually deliver:**
Traditional M&A advisory and business brokerage at scale:
- Exit strategy planning (Plan Smart phase)
- Execution and sale process (Sell Smart phase)
- Complimentary M&A Master Class conference
- Access to their buyer network

**Who it's for:**
Privately-held middle market companies seeking exits up to $1B. Generic — not specific to professional services or expertise businesses.

**What it costs:**
Not disclosed. Traditional M&A advisory fees (retainer + success fee).

**What it does NOT address:**
- Volume brokerage model — likely treats all businesses similarly regardless of whether value is intangible or founder-dependent.
- Website was partially redirecting — domain issues suggest possible organisational changes.
- No indication of specific methodology for founder-centered businesses.
- No coaching or advisory support for the founder's personal transition.

### Business Brokers (IBBA ecosystem)

**What they promise:**
Guided support through every step of buying or selling a business. The IBBA is the world's largest professional community of business intermediaries, with the CBI (Certified Business Intermediary) as the professional credential.

**What they actually deliver:**
Business brokerage: finding buyers, managing confidential marketing, structuring the transaction. CBI designation indicates training in ethics, education, and professionalism.

**Who it's for:**
Small to mid-sized business sellers. Business brokers typically work on smaller deals (under $5M-$10M enterprise value) while M&A advisors handle larger transactions.

**What it costs:**
Typically 8-12% commission on small business sales. Higher success fee percentages at lower deal sizes. Some retainer plus success fee structures.

**What it does NOT address:**
- Most business brokers lack the expertise to value and sell an expertise business where the value is intangible.
- Standard broker approach (CIM, buyer outreach, auction) assumes a business with tangible assets or transferable cash flows — not founder-dependent judgment.
- No pre-sale value creation. They sell what you bring them.
- Key-person risk is typically treated as a discount factor, not something to proactively resolve.

## STRUCTURAL GAPS ACROSS ALL SOLUTIONS

Across all solutions researched, the following gaps are consistent:

**1. The founder-as-product problem is treated as a defect, not a feature.**
Every framework (Value Builder, CEPA, exit planning generally) treats founder-dependency as a risk to be eliminated before sale. None acknowledge that for the target ICP, founder credibility and judgment IS the product — and that the work is to transfer or encode that, not erase it.

**2. Pre-sale preparation stops at operations, not epistemology.**
Solutions focus on making businesses "run without you" through SOPs and systematisation. None address: how do you extract, articulate, and transfer the founder's proprietary methodology and judgment framework so it has standalone value?

**3. No identity or psychological transition support at scale.**
FP Transitions, Succession Plus, and others mention psychological readiness as a concept. The CEPA PREScore exists. But none provide ongoing coaching or structured support for the founder navigating the identity question: "If I am not my firm, who am I?"

**4. No market for the specific ICP.**
The closest is Collective 54 ($5M-$100M boutique professional services, explicitly includes exit track). But it is peer learning — not expert advisory, not transaction execution, and US-only.

**5. Generic frameworks applied to non-generic businesses.**
The Value Acceleration Methodology, CEPA framework, Value Builder system — all are designed to be generic enough to apply to any business. That generality is the problem. An expertise business with £20M revenue built on the founder's reputation and client relationships requires a fundamentally different approach than a product company or trade business.

**6. Transaction execution is late-stage, not integrated.**
M&A advisors (Axial, Oaklyn, Generational) engage when the owner is ready to sell. Exit planning advisors (CEPAs) focus on value building but often lack M&A execution capability. No solution integrates: (a) value creation and IP extraction, (b) founder preparation, and (c) transaction execution as a continuous process.

**7. No solution specifically addresses expertise/knowledge businesses at £5M-£50M in the UK market.**
FP Transitions is the best example of what depth looks like — but it is US-only, RIA-specific, and not applicable. No equivalent exists for consulting firms, advisory practices, or leadership businesses in the UK/European market.
