# Competitive Landscape: AI Transformation for Professional Services Firm Owners

Research date: July 2026. Target buyer: 8-figure founder-owner of an expertise business (consulting, advisory, leadership firm) concerned about AI's impact on firm value, knowledge institutionalisation, and whether to transform before or after exit.

---

## CATEGORY 1: AI ADVISORY / TRANSFORMATION FOR PROFESSIONAL SERVICES FIRMS

### 1.1 Consulting Success — AI Monthly Masterclass Series
**URL:** consultingsuccess.com/ai

**What they promise:**
"Future-proof" consulting practice via AI integration. Claims: 70% research time saved, 3x content output, 30% fee increases.

**What they actually deliver:**
Monthly live webinars hosted by Michel Fortin (Head of Growth, not the founder). Covers "latest AI tools and consulting strategies working right now." Private community, session recordings, implementation guides. Annual VIP adds priority Q&A and quarterly hot-seat sessions.

**Who it's for:**
Individual consultants earning $100K+ annually. Not firm owners managing teams or navigating exit. Individual productivity orientation — how to use ChatGPT faster, not how to transform a firm.

**Cost:**
- $195/month
- $1,950/year (VIP)

**What it does NOT address:**
- Firm-level AI transformation strategy
- Knowledge institutionalisation from founder to system
- Impact on firm valuation or exit multiples
- How AI changes the buyer/acquirer perspective
- Whether to transform before or after exit
- Founder dependency reduction via AI

**Signal:** This is a content subscription, not strategic advisory. The gap between "use AI to write proposals faster" and "restructure your firm around AI" is uncrossed.

### 1.4 Mike Gibbs / SureStep — "Silicon Shell" approach
**Source:** Collective 54 Podcast Ep 252 ("From Billable Hours to AI Leverage")

**What is known:**
Mike Gibbs, founder of SureStep (a professional services firm), discussed transitioning from a billable-hours model to AI-leveraged service delivery on the Collective 54 podcast. The "silicon shell" concept — referenced in community discussions — describes building an AI layer around the firm's core expertise so that the knowledge can be accessed and deployed without the founder's direct involvement in every engagement.

**What is not publicly documented:**
Full methodology, toolset, cost, or whether Gibbs has productised this into a service for others. The podcast episode title suggests the content is primarily a case study from his own firm rather than an advisory offering.

**Signal:** This is an existence proof that boutique firm founders are building AI wrappers around their expertise, but no packaged advisory service built on this approach has been found in public research.

## CATEGORY 2: KNOWLEDGE MANAGEMENT / INSTITUTIONAL KNOWLEDGE PLATFORMS

### 2.1 Guru (getguru.com)
**What they promise:**
Single source of truth for enterprise knowledge. AI-verified knowledge governance. "Transform 60% to 100% of knowledge assessed and verified."

**What they actually deliver:**
Knowledge platform with AI-powered verification agents, enterprise search across tools, role-based access control, audit trails and citations, Slack/Teams/Claude integration. Automated duplicate detection. Knowledge Agent can verify, draft, and surface content.

**Who it's for:**
Enterprise teams and growing companies wanting consistent knowledge access. Primary use case is internal team knowledge management — onboarding, support, sales enablement.

**Cost:** Not published. Contact sales / demo required.

**What it does NOT address:**
Capturing tacit founder knowledge. The system manages structured, already-documented information. It does not extract or systematise expertise from a founder's head. It is a repository management tool, not a knowledge extraction tool. No feature for converting founder judgment into queryable systems. No exit or valuation angle.

---

### 2.2 Tettra
**What they promise:**
AI-powered knowledge base for agencies and professional services. "Provide high-quality, consistent service for every single client." AI bot answers team questions from curated content.

**What they actually deliver:**
Internal wiki with AI-powered Q&A, Slack integration, knowledge verification workflows, subject matter expert assignment for accuracy checks. Law firms and agencies are explicitly listed as use cases. Import from Google Docs and PDFs.

**Who it's for:**
Small to mid-sized teams ($8/user/month minimum 10 users). Agencies, law firms, service businesses wanting consistent team answers.

**Cost:**
- Scaling: $8/user/month (10-user minimum)
- Enterprise: Custom

**What it does NOT address:**
Extracting expertise from a founder's implicit knowledge. Like Guru, Tettra requires information to already be documented before it can manage it. No mechanism for eliciting and structuring tacit expertise. No link to firm valuation or exit readiness. No AI transformation advisory layer.

No link to firm transformation or exit.

## CATEGORY 3: "DIGITAL TWIN" / EXPERT AI PRODUCTS

### 3.1 Delphi.ai
**URL:** delphi.ai

**What they promise:**
"Turn your writing, talks, and frameworks into a digital mind that answers questions on your behalf. At any hour, in your voice." 15-minute setup. Always-on expert presence.

**What they actually deliver:**
AI clone trained on uploaded content (documents, YouTube videos, podcasts, newsletters). Responds to questions in the creator's voice with citations to source material. Chat and voice calling modes. Analytics dashboard. CRM integration. Custom workflows. Embedding on websites.

**Who it's for:**
Experts, coaches, creators, celebrities wanting to scale their reach and answer repetitive questions without personal bandwidth. Example use cases: Matthew Hussey (relationship coach, 2.5M conversations), Harvard Law professor Lawrence Lessig.

**Cost:**
- Free: $0/month (limited usage)
- Builder: $79/month (yearly) — up to 5M training words, 1,000 contacts
- Scaler: $299/month (yearly) — 12M training words, 10,000 contacts, CRM sync, custom domain
- Immortal: Custom — celebrities/public figures, white glove setup, API access

**What it does NOT address:**
Delphi captures the surface of expertise — what an expert has already written or said. It does not extract, structure, or systematise the tacit judgment that has never been articulated. It is a Q&A interface on existing content, not a structured knowledge capture and institutionalisation process. There is no advisory layer, no transformation roadmap, no link to firm operations, team delivery, or exit valuation. Designed for creator/audience dynamics, not firm-internal knowledge transfer. The "digital mind" answers public-facing questions; it does not run a consulting firm or replace founder judgment in complex client engagements.

**Signal gap:** The platform serves public-facing experts who want to scale audience engagement. The use case for a private-firm founder who wants to institutionalise judgment across a delivery team and increase firm value is not served.

## CATEGORY 4: AI STRATEGY FOR PROFESSIONAL SERVICES — NAMED ADVISORS

**Finding:** No named individual advisor, boutique firm, or packaged program was identified in public research that specifically positions itself as an AI strategy advisor for 8-figure professional services firm founders, with a focus on knowledge institutionalisation, exit implications, or firm transformation before/after sale.

The market gap is structural: the advisors who work on exit (M&A advisors, PE firms, value creation consultants) do not address AI. The advisors who address AI (digital transformation consultancies, AI implementation firms) work with large enterprises, not boutique professional services firms. The communities that serve boutique professional services founders (Collective 54) provide peer accountability but not expert delivery or transformation advisory.

**What exists instead:**
- Large consulting firms (Accenture, Deloitte, McKinsey) that address AI transformation but serve enterprise clients, not boutique advisory firms
- Generic AI implementation agencies that deploy tools but have no expertise in professional services firm economics or exit readiness
- Peer communities (Collective 54) where members share experiences but no structured advisory program exists
- Content subscriptions (Consulting Success) for individual consultant productivity, not firm transformation

## CROSS-CUTTING GAPS — What Nothing in the Market Addresses

The following questions have no existing product, program, or advisor visibly serving them:

1. **The economic question:** Does AI transformation before exit increase or decrease sale price for an advisory firm? For whom does it increase? Under what conditions?

2. **The sequencing question:** Should a founder transform the firm and then sell, or sell first and let the acquirer transform? The answer differs by buyer type (strategic vs. PE vs. management buyout) and no one publicly addresses this.

3. **The extraction question:** How do you get a founder's tacit judgment — the contextual pattern recognition that took 20 years to build — into a structured system that can be operated, audited, and ultimately transferred? Existing tools assume knowledge is already documented. The actual problem is that it is not.

4. **The valuation question:** AI-enabled firms that have successfully de-risked founder dependency should command higher multiples. No advisor currently helps founders make that case to buyers or restructure their operations to demonstrate it.

5. **The firm-type specificity:** Expertise businesses (leadership advisory, organisational consulting, executive coaching practices) have different knowledge structures than process firms (IT consulting, finance consulting). The methodology for institutionalising judgment-intensive expertise is fundamentally different from process documentation. No existing solution distinguishes between them.

6. **The before/after frame:** "Transform before exit" vs "transform after exit" is a strategic choice with significant financial and operational implications. No published framework addresses this decision.
