# Existing Solutions: Identity, Transition, and Post-Exit Coaching for 8-Figure Founder-Owners

Research conducted July 2026. Focus: what exists for 8-figure founder-owners of expertise businesses dealing with the identity and psychological dimensions of transition — stepping back, selling, succession, post-exit meaning, and the question of "who am I after this?"

### Strategic Coach (Dan Sullivan)

**Status:** Website returned HTTP 403. Could not retrieve program details.

**What is known from public record:**
Strategic Coach is a long-running (40+ years) coaching program for successful entrepreneurs. Dan Sullivan's core framework is built around the concept of the "Unique Ability" — the entrepreneur's highest-value activity — and the idea that the entrepreneur should focus only on that and delegate everything else. The program explicitly targets high-performing, already-successful founders (not early-stage).

**Key programs (from known public record):**

- *Signature Program* — quarterly workshop days in a peer cohort. Focus on "freeing yourself from obligations that drain you." Emphasis on autonomy, vision, and creating a life where the business serves the founder rather than the reverse.
- *10x Ambitions Program* — for members pursuing 10x goals. Less transition-focused, more growth-focused.
- *Lifetime* — the senior tier, for long-term members who have achieved financial security and are operating in legacy mode.

**What they promise:**
Freedom — specifically, freedom of time, money, relationships, and purpose. The framing is that successful founders get trapped by their own success, and Strategic Coach liberates them by helping them identify what only they can do and building around that.

**Who it's for:**
Established entrepreneurs, typically $1M+ income, committed to the long term (most members stay for years or decades). North American focus, though the program runs globally.

**What it costs:**
Approximately $15,000-$25,000/year depending on program tier and location.

**What it does NOT address:**
- The succession and transition question is implicit but not the core focus. Strategic Coach is about optimising the founder's ongoing productive life, not about exit.
- No specific framework for encoding or transferring founder IP.
- No explicit "post-exit identity" program. The assumption is that the founder keeps going, just with more freedom.
- No AI strategy component.
- The identity question is addressed indirectly through "Unique Ability" but not as a transition challenge.

### Vistage

**Status:** Fetched. No specific transition or exit programming found.

**What they deliver:**
Vistage provides peer advisory groups (typically 12-16 members) led by a Vistage Chair, who is a retired or semi-retired executive serving as the group facilitator and monthly 1:1 coach. Monthly full-day group meetings plus monthly 1:1 coaching session with the Chair.

**Who it's for:**
CEOs and business owners broadly. Peer groups are not segmented by transition stage. Some Chairs may have personal expertise in exits or transitions, but this is Chair-dependent, not a Vistage program.

**What it costs:**
Monthly dues (undisclosed publicly) plus a one-time enrolment fee. Industry estimates put it at $1,000-$2,000/month depending on program type.

**What it does NOT address:**
- No programming specifically for founders in exit or identity transition.
- The peer group format means the value depends heavily on the other members' relevance to your situation. A post-exit founder in a room of growth-stage operators gets limited signal.
- No structured identity or psychological support. The format is peer advisory, not therapy or deep coaching.
- No AI strategy or IP monetisation content.

### YPO (Young Presidents Organization)

**Status:** Fetched. No specific exit or transition programming confirmed.

**What they deliver:**
YPO is described as "the world's largest leadership community of chief executives — over 38,000 extraordinary global members." Monthly chapter meetings, forums (small groups of 8-10 peers meeting monthly in confidential settings), global events, speaker programmes, and educational content. The forum model is the core: peer confidentiality and emotional support.

**Who it's for:**
Chief executives and business owners under 45 at time of joining (age limit), though members can remain as they age. Minimum company size and personal requirements for membership.

**What it costs:**
Joining fees and annual dues vary by chapter. Typically $5,000-$15,000/year plus chapter fees. Wealthier chapters can be significantly more.

**What it does NOT address:**
- YPO is for active leaders running companies. The community largely dissolves in relevance once a founder exits — there is no formal YPO post-exit track.
- The forum model provides peer support but no expert guidance on transition or succession.
- No structured identity coaching, IP monetisation, or AI strategy content.
- Age restriction at entry means this skews younger than the target ICP.

### Tiger 21

**Status:** Fetched in detail.

**What they promise:**
"Where Success Becomes More Meaningful — and Impactful." A peer advisory network for ultra-high-net-worth individuals. The positioning is explicitly for people who have already made their money and are asking: now what?

**What they actually deliver:**
- Monthly peer group meetings (12-15 members) — confidential, in-person, facilitated
- Specialty interest networks (T21 Connect Networks) — specific topics like real estate, family office, philanthropy
- Quarterly asset allocation reports and market insights
- Global events and networking
- Focus areas: wealth preservation, family legacy, philanthropic impact

**Who it's for:**
Individuals with $20M+ qualifying net worth (excluding personal use assets). Members are typically post-liquidity — they have sold a company, inherited wealth, or made exits. The community is explicitly positioned for the post-exit stage.

**What it costs:**
Membership fees not publicly disclosed. Industry estimates suggest $30,000-$50,000/year. The $20M minimum effectively segments to genuinely wealthy individuals.

**What it does NOT address:**
- Tiger 21 is focused on wealth management and preservation, not identity or meaning. The peer discussion is about investment portfolios, real estate, and philanthropy — not "who am I now."
- No coaching element. Peer-only support.
- No AI strategy, IP monetisation, or succession planning content.
- No structured support for the psychological dimension of post-exit loss of identity.
- The community skews toward pure wealth-holders. Founders who have exited but want to remain active and build something new may find the orientation too passive.

**Key gap:** The $20M floor means many 8-figure founders ($10M-$19M net worth) don't qualify. And even for those who do, the offering is wealth-stage optimisation, not founder-transition support.

### EO (Entrepreneurs' Organization)

**Status:** Fetched. No specific exit or transition programming found.

**What they deliver:**
Three tracks: Established Entrepreneurs (main chapters), Early Stage (accelerator), Students. Core offering is chapter membership with peer forums (similar to YPO format), executive education, events. The forum model — small groups meeting monthly in confidentiality — is the primary value.

**Who it's for:**
Entrepreneurs with businesses meeting minimum revenue thresholds ($1M+ depending on market). Active business owners.

**What it does NOT address:**

- No coaching or expert guidance. Peer-only.
- No structured identity, IP, or succession content.

### Built to Sell / PREScore

**Status:** Fetched.

The PREScore (Personal Readiness to Exit) is the only exit-preparation tool found in this research that explicitly addresses psychological readiness. It identifies whether a founder is emotionally prepared to exit, drawing on research that 74% of owners have regrets one year after exiting.

**What it does:**
Online assessment. Results-only. No coaching, no program, no follow-through.

**What it does NOT address:**
Assessment only. The score identifies whether you're ready — it does not help you become ready or support you through the transition.

## STRUCTURAL GAP ANALYSIS

### Does any single solution address the combination of (AI strategy + succession + identity + institutional knowledge + legacy)?

**No. None found.**

The closest candidates and why they fall short:

| Solution | AI Strategy | Succession | Identity | IP Encoding | Legacy | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic Coach | No | Implicit | Partially (Unique Ability) | No | Implicit | Built for ongoing active founders, not transition |
| Tiger 21 | No | No | No | No | Partially (philanthropy) | Wealth stage only, no founder identity work |
| Collective 54 | No | Yes (exit track) | No | No | No | US-only peer community, no individual advisory |
| Thought Leadership Leverage | No | No | No | Partially | No | Builds new TL practice, doesn't extract/transfer existing IP |
| Built to Sell / PREScore | No | Partially | Assessment only | No | No | Assessment without follow-through |
| Beyond the Exit (Rood) | No | Partially | Yes | No | No | Post-exit only, no pre-succession preparation, site appears inactive |
| Jo Swann | No | No | Yes | No | No | Therapy only, not advisory, appears small-scale practice |

### Five consistent gaps across all solutions

**1. The AI strategy blind spot is total.**
No solution in the market addresses the question of how AI changes what a founder should do with their knowledge, their role, or their succession. Not a single program, community, or coaching framework has integrated AI strategy into founder transition advice. This is a gap as of July 2026 — despite AI being the defining business context for the past three years.

**2. Identity transition has no premium home.**
The closest offerings are Jo Swann (UK psychotherapist, small practice, site down) and John Rood (Beyond the Exit, podcast-based, site appears inactive). Tiger 21 and YPO provide peer connection but not structured identity support. Strategic Coach addresses autonomy but not transition. There is no premium, structured, expert-led program specifically for the psychological and identity dimensions of founder transition.

**3. Institutional knowledge encoding is not a service that exists at scale.**
There is no advisory product that says: "We will help you extract, structure, and transfer your accumulated judgment, frameworks, and proprietary methodology so that it has standalone value — whether you're selling it, licensing it, embedding it in a successor, or encoding it in AI." Thought Leadership Leverage is the closest but points in the wrong direction (building, not transferring) and does not address the AI dimension.

**4. The "who am I next?" question is unaddressed by premium offerings.**
Tiger 21, YPO, and Vistage provide peer community. Strategic Coach provides framework. But none offer structured expert guidance on identity reconstruction — what does the founder who has built a 20-year business around their name and judgment do with themselves after the business transitions? The market treats this as a personal therapy problem (Jo Swann) or ignores it entirely.

**5. Pre-exit, during-transition, and post-exit are siloed.**
Solutions either prepare you to exit (Built to Sell, ExitDNA, CEPA ecosystem), execute the transaction (M&A advisors), or support post-exit wealth management (Tiger 21). No solution accompanies a founder through the full arc: from "I am thinking about stepping back" through "I have sold / transitioned" to "I am building my next act." The psychological reality is that these phases are connected and interdependent — but the market treats them as separate products.

---

## MARKET STRUCTURE SUMMARY

The market for 8-figure founder transition is fragmented across four archetypes:

**Wealth managers and M&A advisors** — serve the financial transaction well. Ignore the human.

**Peer communities (YPO, Tiger 21, Vistage, EO)** — serve the ongoing social network. No structured guidance on the transition questions specifically. Quality depends on who else is in the room.

**Exit planning advisors (CEPA ecosystem, Value Builder)** — serve the operational de-risking. Generic frameworks that treat the founder-as-product problem as a defect.

**Coaches and therapists (Jo Swann, Strategic Coach, John Rood)** — the only ones addressing the identity and psychological dimension. But they are small-scale, fragmented, and none integrate business strategy with personal transition.

**Nobody** integrates all five dimensions: AI strategy, succession planning, identity transition, institutional knowledge transfer, and legacy design into a single coherent advisory offering for 8-figure founder-owners of expertise businesses.

That is the gap.
