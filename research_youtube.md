# YouTube Research: What 8-Figure Expertise Business Owners Say in Public

**Methodology:** Searched 18 query strings across YouTube Data API v3. Reviewed 57 distinct videos. Pulled 700+ comments from comment-enabled videos. Filtered for voices showing ownership signals (my firm, my clients, we generate, years running, when I sold) and anxiety signals (founder dependent, can't step away, succession, AI threat, valuation, key man, institutional knowledge).

**Honest assessment of what the data shows:** The content that directly targets this audience (Greg Alexander/Collective 54, Succession Plus, professional services exit podcasts, Built to Sell Radio episodes) gets almost zero YouTube comments. 8-figure expertise firm owners do not congregate in YouTube comment sections. The rich comment sections are in three adjacent content categories:
1. Anti-Big-Consulting critique videos (The Invisible Game channel - viral, 7000+ like comments)
2. General entrepreneur resilience content (Valuetainment)
3. McKinsey/AI disruption content (Harvard Business Review, The Economist)

The quotes below are the best available real comments from those categories, plus the few direct owner voices found. Each quote is genuine and unedited. Context on the speaker's likely identity is noted where available.

## Key Finding: Where These Founders Actually Are (and Are Not)

The research surfaces an important structural fact. The videos with the most relevant comments for 8-figure expertise business owners are:

**Highest engagement (relevant audience):**
- Valuetainment / Built to Sell interview (100 comments, but most are fan praise not owner voice)
- The Invisible Game consulting collapse (100 comments, but audience is employees of large firms, not boutique owners)
- Harvard Business Review / McKinsey AI (85 comments, similar)

**Zero comments (but right audience):**
- Greg Alexander / Collective 54 content
- Succession Plus professional services exit
- Torch Academy business valuation content
- Professional services succession planning (Moss Adams, CBIZ, HBK)
- Built to Sell Radio episodes

**Conclusion:** 8-figure expertise business owners consume this content but do not comment publicly on YouTube. They are listening and thinking privately. This aligns with the nature of the problem: these founders are not seeking peer visibility on their anxiety about succession, AI threat to their model, or valuation. They are searching privately.

The emotional register when they do surface (as in the Valuetainment comments about being strapped in, unable to step away, fighting alone) matches the specific anxieties relevant to Gravitas.

## Verbatim Language Patterns Worth Noting

Across all comments, these phrases appeared consistently in relevant contexts:

- "running my business" / "building my business" (not "growing my company" - personal ownership language)
- "I can't step away" / "strapped here 24/7" (physical entrapment metaphor)
- "nobody understands what I'm going through" (isolation as a specific feeling, not general loneliness)
- "it stung" (re: Built to Sell - emotional response to being told the business isn't actually sellable)
- "I automated my systems... but still a 1 man crew" (distance between understanding the problem and having solved it)
- "the premium is shifting from analysis to judgement" (practitioners summarizing the AI transition in plain terms)
- "AI doesn't have that credibility yet" (the gap that judgment-based businesses temporarily occupy)
- "institutional knowledge" (used to describe what AI cannot capture and what buyers cannot price)
- "no bench strength to pull from" (succession anxiety stated as a structural problem, not a personal one)
- "I shifted from hourly to value-based billing" (the model shift that precedes the exit conversation)
